# Getting Started with GTM Strategy Frameworks

## First 5 Minutes

### Test 1: EPIC Motion Diagnostic

"We are a Series B logistics SaaS company selling to enterprise supply chain teams. $2M ARR, 120% NRR, 90-day sales cycle, 80% of deals come from outbound. Which GTM motion should lead?"

You should see: Scores for all 4 motions (E/P/I/C), a recommended lead motion, Monday-morning action items, and stage-appropriate guidance.

### Test 2: IMPACT Quick Positioning

"We sell procurement automation to mid-market manufacturers in India. We keep losing to Coupa in competitive deals. Buyers say we sound like everyone else. Help me position."

You should see: Anchor Market analysis (sub-category vs. category vs. new category), a 4-level messaging hierarchy (core message, elevator pitch, full narrative, detailed story), and buyer language over internal language.

### Test 3: CRAFT Context Engineering

"I asked Claude to build a competitive analysis but the output was too generic. Here was my prompt: 'analyze our competitors.' What am I missing?"

You should see: A CRAFT audit identifying which elements are missing (Character, Result, Artifact, Frame, Timeline), a rewritten prompt with all 5 elements, and the before/after quality difference explained.

## Tips

Name your company, stage, and industry. Generic questions produce generic answers. The more context you give, the more specific the scoring and recommendations.

State the actual problem, not the symptom. "Our funnel is broken" is a symptom. "We generate 200 MQLs/month but only 3% convert to SQL" is the problem.

Ask for the Monday action. After the diagnostic, say "what should I do first thing Monday?" The skill will give you a single, concrete action.
